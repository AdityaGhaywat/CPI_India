#  CPI & Inflation Analysis — India (Excel Case Study)

##  Problem Statement
You are working with India's National Statistical Office to analyze CPI (Consumer Price Index) data.  
The objective is to identify inflation trends, category contributions, and external factors affecting price movements.

---

##  Dataset
- CPI data across multiple categories (Food, Healthcare, Essential Services, etc.)
- Time period includes pre-COVID, COVID, and post-COVID phases  
- Includes monthly, yearly, and category-level inflation metrics  

---

##  Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel  
- Pivot Tables  
- Charts (Line, Bar, Pie)  
- Correlation Analysis  
- Basic formulas (MoM %, YoY %, aggregation)

---

##  Business Questions & Insights

---

### 1️⃣ Which category contributes most to CPI?

![CPI Contribution](Dashboards/CPI%20Contribution.png)

**Insight:**
- Food category contributes the highest (~44%)
- Food + Essential Services together account for **60%+ of CPI**
- Inflation is heavily driven by essential consumption

---

### 2️⃣ Year-on-Year CPI Trend (Post 2017)

![YoY Trend](Dashboards/YoY%20Growth%20Analysis.png)

**Insight:**
- Highest inflation spike observed in **2019–2020 (~16.3%)**
- Key drivers:
  - Food inflation (crop damage, monsoon issues)
  - Rupee depreciation (import cost increase)
  - Supply chain disruptions (COVID onset)

---

### 3️⃣ Food Inflation (Last 12 Months Analysis)

![Food Inflation](Dashboards/Inflation%20in%20a%20year%20for%20Food%20Category.png)

**Insight:**
- Highest inflation: Apr–May 2023 (~0.78%)
- Lowest inflation: Nov–Dec 2022 (~-0.70%)
- Biggest contributor: **Meat & Fish category**
- Absolute change over period: ~0.60%

---

### 4️⃣ Impact of COVID-19 on Inflation

![COVID Impact](Dashboards/Covid-19%20Impact.png)

**Insight:**
- Food inflation spiked due to supply chain disruptions
- Healthcare inflation steadily increased (high demand)
- Essential services became highly volatile
- Inflation became **category-specific post COVID**

---

### 5️⃣ Impact of Crude Oil on Inflation (2021–2023)

![Crude Oil](Dashboards/Crude%20Oil%20Effects.png)

**Insight:**
- 2021: Strong positive correlation (especially clothing category ~0.88)
- 2022: Weak correlation across categories
- 2023: Negative correlation trend observed
- Relationship between oil and inflation is **dynamic, not constant**


---

##  Key Takeaways
- Inflation in India is largely driven by essential categories  
- External shocks (COVID, oil prices) significantly alter inflation patterns  
- Relationships between variables (like oil & inflation) are not static  
- Data-driven analysis helps uncover **hidden economic patterns**
